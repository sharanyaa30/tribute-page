A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

15 October 1931 – 27 July 2015

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was an Indian aerospace scientist and statesman who served as the president of India from 2002 to 2007. Born and raised in a Muslim family in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, Kalam studied physics and aerospace engineering. He spent the next four decades as a scientist and science administrator, mainly at the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) and was intimately involved in India's civilian space programme and military missile development efforts. He was known as the "Missile Man of India" for his work on the development of ballistic missile and launch vehicle technology. He also played a pivotal organisational, technical, and political role in Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998, India's second such test after the first test in 1974.

Key Achievements

  • Led India’s first satellite launch (SLV-III, 1980)

    • In 1980, Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam led India’s first successful satellite launch as Project Director of SLV-III. The mission placed the Rohini RS-1 satellite into orbit, making India the sixth nation with satellite launch capability. This achievement marked a major milestone in India’s space program and showcased Kalam’s leadership and technical vision.

  • Directed development of Agni and Prithvi missiles

    • Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam directed the development of India’s Agni and Prithvi missiles under the Integrated Guided Missile Development Programme. Agni was designed for long-range strategic defense, while Prithvi served as a short-range battlefield missile. His leadership in these projects earned him the title “Missile Man of India.”

  • Played a pivotal role in Pokhran-II nuclear tests (1998)

    • Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam played a key role in India’s Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998 as the chief scientific coordinator. He worked closely with defense and atomic energy teams to ensure the successful execution of five underground nuclear explosions. His leadership helped demonstrate India’s nuclear capabilities and strengthened its strategic position globally.

  • Served as the 11th President of India (2002–2007)

    • Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. Widely known as the “People’s President,” he brought a unique blend of scientific vision and humility to the role. During his tenure, he focused on youth engagement, education, and national development, often visiting schools and universities to inspire students. He also advocated for his PURA (Providing Urban Amenities in Rural Areas) model to bridge the urban-rural divide. His presidency was marked by accessibility, integrity, and a deep commitment to public service.
